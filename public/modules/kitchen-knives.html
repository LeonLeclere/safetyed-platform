Section 2 of 5 · Standard Operating Procedure
Order of Safe Operation
The 10-Step Safe Knife SOP — Study This First

Every knife session in Food Technology follows this exact sequence — from before you touch anything to after everything is stored. Read each step and why it matters before you attempt the ordering activity below.

1
💧 Wash and dry hands thoroughly
20 seconds minimum with soap. Hand contamination is the #1 cause of food-borne illness. Always first — before touching any equipment.
2
👕 Put on PPE: apron, tie hair back, remove jewellery
PPE before equipment — every time. Hair in food = contamination. Jewellery = injury and bacterial contamination risk.
3
🟩 Select a chopping board and inspect it for damage
Check for deep grooves or cracks — bacteria live in damaged board surfaces. A damaged board must be replaced before use.
4
🪣 Place damp cloth under board — confirm it does not slide
Engineering control. A sliding board is the most common cause of knife lacerations in school kitchens. Press down and test before cutting.
5
🔪 Inspect knife: blade integrity, handle security, cleanliness
Check for chips, bends, loose handle. Defective equipment must be removed from service and reported — never used.
6
✋ Demonstrate claw grip to teacher and receive sign-off
Your teacher must observe your claw grip and confirm you are ready before your first cut. This applies every single session — not just once.
7
🥦 Prepare food item: create flat surface, place stable side down
Round or unstable items must be stabilised before cutting. Cut a flat base first. Food that rolls or slips during cutting is a direct injury risk.
8
✂️ Cut with claw grip, controlled pressure, blade below knuckles
Maintain the claw grip throughout. Never look away from the blade. If you lose focus or technique — knife goes on the board immediately.
9
⬇️ Put knife down on board when not actively cutting
Any pause, distraction, conversation or movement = knife on board first. Never carry a knife unless you are actively transporting it to a destination.
10
🧼 Wash knife individually, dry immediately, return to storage
Never submerge in a full sink. Hold by handle, wipe blade away from edge. Dry immediately. Return to knife block or roll — blade always enclosed in storage.

Section 4 of 5 · PPE & Equipment Check
Personal Protective Equipment
What to Wear — Before You Pick Up Any Knife

All items below are required before any knife activity. Check yourself before you check your equipment.

🥼
Apron
Tied and covering your front. Provides a physical barrier and keeps clothing contamination-free.
👟
Closed-toe shoes
A dropped knife falls point-down. Open-toed footwear provides zero protection. No thongs, sandals, or bare feet.
💇
Hair tied back
All hair secured off the face and neck. Loose hair near a blade is both a contamination and injury risk.
💍
All jewellery removed
Rings, bracelets, and watches must come off — not just moved up the wrist. They harbour bacteria and catch on equipment.
💪
Sleeves rolled up
Rolled above the elbow before you begin. Loose sleeves can catch on equipment or drag across food.
🥽
Safety goggles — NOT required
Not required for standard knife tasks. Do not wear them — they are not designed for food tech hazards and create a false sense of security.
Remember: PPE is your last layer of protection — not your first. Correct technique and a stable workspace come before PPE in the safety hierarchy. Wear all required items AND use correct technique. One does not replace the other.
